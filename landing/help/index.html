Help & FAQ

Find answers to common questions about Terbium, plans, features, and environments.

What are the available plans?
Terbium offers three plans:
  • Free: Core features, 5-minute session limit, no login required.
  • Pro: Unlimited session time, advanced features, unlock with a Pro or Beta key.
  • Ultimate: All features, owner server, custom environments, and more.
See full plans & pricing
What features are locked for Pro or Ultimate?
Some advanced features (like unlimited session time, certain apps, and integrations) are only available on Pro or Ultimate. Free users are limited to 5-minute sessions and core features.
How does the Free session limit work?
Free users can use Terbium for up to 5 minutes per session. To continue, upgrade to Pro or enter a Beta/Pro key. Ultimate users have no time limits.
How do I unlock Pro or Beta features?
Enter a valid Pro or Beta key in the app to unlock advanced features and remove session limits.
Can I create custom environments?
Yes! You can create your own custom environments. Optionally, you can require authentication for your custom environment for extra security.
Is authentication required?
No login is required for Free or Pro. For custom environments, you can enable authentication if you wish.
What subdomains and pages exist?
Here are the main subdomains and pages you can visit:
  • / — Main app (redirects to /landing/)
  • /landing/ — Landing page
  • /plans/ — Plans & pricing
  • /docs/setup-guide — Setup guide
  • /help/ — This help & FAQ page
  • /admin/panel/ — Admin panel (Ultimate/owner only)
  • /index.html — Main app UI
  • /404.html — Not found page
  • /dev/ — Dev environment (if enabled)
  • /beta/ — Beta environment (if enabled)
  • /sync/ — Sync server (Ultimate/owner only)
  • /tb.svg, /tb-landing.svg, /tb-ultimate.svg, etc. — App icons
Some subdomains or pages may require a specific plan or key.
Is Terbium open-source?
The core site is static and extendable. Contact us for more details about open-source options.
Will it work offline?
Many features work offline via service workers and caching.
